Personal Projects

The Moon

The Moon is a top-down adventure game, which depicts an ordinary day in a life of a busy girl as a student.

An unexpected day comes when she gets teleported to another dimension, where she meets the love of her life.

The style, gameplay and feel was motivated by Undertale, since it is one of my favorite games.

Code, art, sounds and everything was done by my from ground up.

I had to learn the GameMaker script, how the engine worked and other things to construct this on the GMS2 engine.

It is yet to be finished, since January, I have created:

  • Basic movement and interaction
  • Dialogue system
  • Cutscene-like sequences
  • Art and Sounds

The art and setting that I envisioned is quite extensive, as the story will take a turn

and transport you to a different dimension. After I create the art, I'd like finalize it.

Here is a small demo video of current build.

Team Projects

EosEngine

EosEngine is a game engine that was created by a team of 4 people at my university, including me.

It was done using LWJGL library on Java. I mostly was on the graphical side of things.

I was responsible for:

  • Implementing the whole rendering pipeline
  • Object loading
  • Texturing
  • Lighting
  • Random-height terrain generation
  • General improvements/optimizations in the engine core

You can find the source code here

Galaxies, please!

Galaxies, please! is a "Papers, please!"-esque game, in which you are tasked to identify galaxies correctly, given an image taken by the JWST.

This was created on a NASA Space Apps Hackathon 2022 alongside 3 other people. I used PyGame library to program the game.

I was responsible for programming the whole gameplay and game loop.
